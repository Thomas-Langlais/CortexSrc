HORIZONTAL REVIEW OF SKILLS PROGRAMMING
PROGRAM INVENTORY
PHASE 1 REPORTING TEMPLATE

APPROACH

Overview

Budget 2018 announced that the Government of Canada would be undertaking a horizontal reivew of skills programming (the Review) over the next year, "to maximize the effectiveness of these programs, particulatly in the way that they offer support to workers wishing to take advantage of emerging opportunities." The review is intended to be broad, encompassing "a wide range of skills programming to meet a variety of needs." In general terms, the Review would include all Government of Canada programming designed to support an adaptable and inclsuive workforce capable of meeting labour market demands now and in the future.

A core component of the Review is the collection and analysis of data on the Government of Canada's skills development programs. Treasury Board Secretariat (TBS) is undertaking the collection of program data using a two phased approach:

  1. In the Phase 1 template, departments are asked to answer questions that will be used to determine whether each individual program should be included within the Review (i.e., determining which programs are 'in-scope'), and to provide basic program and financial information.
  2. Based on the results from Phase 1, the Task Team will establish a final program inventory for the Review to assess in-depth. In the Phase 2 template, which will come later, departments will be required to provide additional information on skills-related programs and/or services (including performance/results information and service delivery details).
